About this node

SWNE-GRVW is a Meshtastic repeater installed at the Green's View overlook in Sewanee, Tennessee, on the edge of the Cumberland Plateau — one of the area's most scenic and exposed high points, making it an ideal location for a radio node.

Meshtastic / LoRa

This node operates as a repeater: it does not send or receive direct messages itself, but automatically relays any message it hears to extend the reach of the local mesh for every Meshtastic user in the area.

Because it is solar powered, SWNE-GRVW runs continuously — day and night, through the seasons — without any maintenance. If you're in or near Sewanee with a Meshtastic device, this node is quietly working in the background, making sure your messages have the best possible chance of getting through.

Sewanee Mesh

Part of a larger network

SWNE-GRVW is one node in the growing Sewanee Mesh. Its position at the Green's View overlook gives it excellent line-of-sight across the plateau, making it a strong relay point for connecting other nodes and users in the area.
